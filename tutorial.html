Tutorial



Step 1

Collect your tools, including a lock, a pick, and a tension wrench.


Step 2

Place the tension wrench into the key cylinder, usually the point furthest away from the pins.


Step 3

Insert a pick into the lock, feeling the pins inside the lock.


Step 4

Gently touch the tension wrench in the direction the key cylinder turns and slowly push the individual pins.


Step 5

Eventually the pins will set and the lock will turn and release, if the pins get stuck take pressure off the tension wrench and try again.

Tips

• Some locks may have rusted pins and are almost impossible to open without a key or severely damaging the lock
• Try different picks and picking techniques all locks are slightly different on the inside!
• If you have the key for the lock you are trying to open try comparing the shape of the key to find the right position for the pins.
